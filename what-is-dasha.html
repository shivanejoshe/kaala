Vedic Timing System

What is Vimshottari Dasha?

A 120-year planetary period system anchored to your Moon's nakshatra position at birth. Each planet governs a defined span of your life. The sequence is fully deterministic — computable from a single moment in time.

The basic structure

Vimshottari Dasha divides human life into nine planetary periods totalling 120 years. Each planet rules a period of fixed length. The sequence always runs in the same order — Ketu, Venus, Sun, Moon, Mars, Rahu, Jupiter, Saturn, Mercury — but where you enter the sequence depends entirely on your Moon's position at birth.

Specifically, it depends on which of the 27 nakshatras (lunar mansions) your Moon occupied at the moment of your birth, and how far through that nakshatra the Moon had traveled. This determines both which Mahadasha you begin life in and how much of that period remains at birth.

Three levels of timing

The system nests three levels deep:

Mahadasha — the major period. The planet that governs the broadest sweep of your current life phase. A Venus Mahadasha runs for 20 years; a Sun Mahadasha for 6.

Antardasha — the sub-period within the Mahadasha. Each Mahadasha is subdivided into nine Antardashas, one for each planet, in the same sequence. Their durations are proportional to the Mahadasha length.

Pratyantar — the sub-sub-period. Each Antardasha is further divided into nine Pratyantars. These run for weeks to a few months.

At any given moment, you are simultaneously in a Mahadasha, Antardasha, and Pratyantar. The specific combination of all three is what makes a particular window distinctive. A Saturn Mahadasha / Venus Antardasha period has a very different character from a Saturn Mahadasha / Rahu Antardasha period — even though both are within the same major period.

Why this matters for decisions

Career moves, business launches, major financial commitments, and relationship decisions all have different structural outcomes depending on which dasha layer is active when they occur. This is not superstition — it is the observation that the same person, with the same skills, gets structurally different outcomes at different points in their cycle. The dasha system is the framework for reading that cycle.

What makes a period "good" or "difficult"

A planet's delivery during its Mahadasha depends on several factors: its natural benefic or malefic nature, its functional role for your specific ascendant (which houses it lords), its placement in your natal chart, and the planets it's associated with or aspected by.

The same planet behaves differently for different people. Saturn is considered a natural malefic — but for a Taurus or Libra ascendant, Saturn lords trines and is the chart's Yogakaraka (most benefic planet). A Saturn period for these ascendants often marks the most important career-building years of their life.

This is why generic period interpretations ("Rahu period is always difficult") are unreliable. The specific outcome of a period is computed from the whole chart, not from the planet alone.
